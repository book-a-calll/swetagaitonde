Common Questions

Frequently Asked

A hair transplant moves healthy follicles from the donor area (back/sides of scalp) to balding areas, resulting in permanent, natural hair regrowth. FUE is the most advanced minimally-invasive technique.

Most sessions last 4–8 hours depending on the technique and number of grafts required. You're awake throughout with local anaesthesia — most patients watch movies or relax.

New hair growth typically begins within 3–4 months. Full, dense, natural results are visible by 12 months. The transplanted hair is permanent and continues to grow naturally.

The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia, so discomfort is minimal. Most patients describe only mild pressure during the initial numbing. Post-procedure soreness is manageable with standard medication.
